Cyber Attacks

  • Hacker attacks NASA and the U.S. Department of Defense.

    1999

    Jonathan James was only 15 years old when he repeatedly infiltrated the U.S. Department of Defense and the National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA) in 1999. During the attack against the Defense Department's Threat Reduction Agency, an office charged with countering nuclear, biological and chemical weapons threats, he stole usernames and passwords and more than 3,000 emails.

  • North Korea's alleged attack on Sony

    2014

    In November 2014, Sony Pictures suffered a cyber attack after a group of hackers calling themselves Guardians of Peace gained access to the company's computer network. North Korea denied responsibility, but described the attack as a "just action" in response to Sony's film "The Interview," a comedy depicting the violent death of North Korea's Kim Jong-un.

  • Attack on Ukrainian power grid

    2015

    Ukraine's security service blamed the Russian government for the attack. On the other hand, without naming Moscow, some U.S. private security companies that investigated the event said they believed it originated in Russia. The attack is believed to be the first time hackers have been able to successfully attack an electricity distribution network.

  • U.S. presidential election

    2016

    Hackers leaked thousands of emails from the Democratic National Committee (DNC), the Democratic Party's board of directors, during the 2016 presidential election. The leak embarrassed the party leadership, who expressed disdain in some emails for the campaign of Bernie Sanders, a candidate who had competed with Hillary Clinton to become the party's presidential nominee.

  • WannaCry

    2017

    A ransomware attack known as WannaCry infected some 300,000 computers in 150 countries in May 2017. The software encrypted files and demanded users hand over hundreds of dollars in exchange for keys to decrypt the files. The attack affected hospitals, including many belonging to the UK's National Health Service (NHS), banks and other companies. The FedEx company said it had lost hundreds of millions of dollars as a result of the attack. The U.S. and U.K. blamed North Korea, an accusation Pyongyang denied and called a "serious political provocation."
